What is Forex & the Pip

Currency pairs, pips, and what "1.2500 → 1.2550" actually means.
▼

Forex = trading one currency against another. XAU/USD means gold priced in US dollars.

Pip = the smallest standard price move. For most pairs, 1 pip = 0.0001. For XAU/USD, 1 pip = $0.10 move.

Example: Gold moves $2500.00 → $2502.50. That's 25 pips ($2.50 worth).

Why it matters: You size trades in pips. A 30-pip stop on gold = $3 of price movement per 0.01 lot.

QUIZ — M1
Gold moves from 2500.00 → 2503.00. How many pips?
XAU/USD: 1 pip = 0.10. A $3 move = 30 pips.
XAU/USD stands for?
XAU = gold (chemical symbol Au, X prefix = metal), USD = US dollar quote.
A 30-pip stop-loss on 0.01 lot XAU/USD risks how much?
0.01 lot = 1 ounce. 1 pip = $0.01 P/L on 0.01 lot × 30 pips ≈ $0.30 per cent of move. 30 pips = 300 cents = $3.

Bid / Ask / Spread

Why you enter at a worse price than quoted.
▼

Bid = what buyers pay. Ask = what sellers ask. Spread = the gap.

Gold spread is ~15-30 cents in London hours, widens to $1+ at NY close and on news.

You start every trade at a loss equal to the spread. Price must move in your favour by the spread before you break even.

Rule: Never trade 30 min before / after NFP, CPI, FOMC — spread balloons and slippage destroys your stops.

QUIZ — M3

Spread + slippage can turn a 30-pip SL into 80 pips realised. Skip it.

You can profit from falling markets too.
▼

LONG (BUY): profit when price rises. Enter low, exit high.

Stop Loss & Take Profit

Where to put them, and why moving them kills accounts.
▼

SL placement: just beyond the structure — above the high (for shorts) or below the low (for longs). ATR × 1.5 is a reliable default for gold on 1H.

TP placement: minimum 1:2 RR (twice your risk). On a 30-pip stop → 60-pip target.

Do NOT move SL further away. Ever. It's the #1 account killer — "just a bit more" becomes -5% in one trade.

Moving SL closer (to break-even once price moves 1R in your favour) is fine and encouraged.

Momentum oscillator. 0-100 scale.
▼

Measures recent gains vs recent losses over 14 bars. Scale: 0 (fully sold) to 100 (fully bought).

RSI < 30: oversold — bounce likely. In PROSPER we use < 40 for faster signals.

Bollinger Bands

Volatility envelope — when to expect reversals.
▼

A 20-period moving average ± 2 standard deviations. Creates an upper and lower band that expands in volatile markets and contracts in quiet ones.

Price touching lower band + RSI oversold = statistically likely bounce (in uptrends).

Price touching upper band + RSI overbought = statistically likely pullback (in downtrends).

BB squeeze: bands contract tightly → big move coming. Don't trade during squeeze — wait for the breakout direction to confirm.

Middle band (20 MA) often acts as dynamic support/resistance. Good place to trail stops to.

QUIZ — M8
BB standard settings are...
Bollinger’s original and still the default everywhere.
Bands squeeze tight together. Best action?
Squeeze = low volatility before expansion. Direction unknown until break.
Middle band (20 MA) is useful as...
In trends, price respects the 20 MA. Trail stops just the other side of it.

Order Types

Market, limit, stop, trailing — when to use each.
▼

Market order: fill now at current price. Use for confirmed breakouts.

Limit order (buy-limit/sell-limit): enter at a better price than current. Buy-limit = below current price (pullback entry).

Stop order (buy-stop/sell-stop): enter at a worse price than current. Buy-stop = above current (breakout confirmation).

Trailing stop: SL follows price by fixed distance. Locks in profit without manual management. Use on runners.

PROSPER preference: limit orders for pullback entries (less FOMO), market for confirmed signal bars only.

Stop orders confirm momentum (worse price). Sell-stop below current = breakout short.
Trailing stop best used on...
Trailing gets chopped in ranges and spiked off in news. Good for runners.

Support & Resistance

Horizontal levels where price has reacted. Your map.
▼

Support: price level where buyers stepped in before. Price tends to bounce here on revisit.

Resistance: price level where sellers stepped in before. Price tends to stall on revisit.

Role reversal: broken support becomes resistance (and vice versa). Powerful re-entry zone.

How to mark: use the 4H chart. Find 3+ touches. Draw at wicks (liquidity) not candle bodies. Clean levels only.

Gold-specific: psychological round numbers (2500, 2550) are high-reaction zones. Pre-mark them.

Candlestick Patterns

Pin bar, engulfing, doji — reversal signals that work.
▼

Pin bar: long wick, small body. Rejection of a level. Wick up = sellers won → short. Wick down = buyers won → long.

Bullish engulfing: large green candle fully swallows prior red body. Reversal after a downtrend.

Bearish engulfing: opposite. Large red swallows prior green. Reversal after uptrend.

Doji: open = close. Indecision. Only meaningful at S/R levels — standalone = noise.

Critical: candlesticks only matter at key levels (S/R, trend line, EMA). Patterns in the middle of nowhere = ignore.

QUIZ — M11

Doji forming in the middle of a range with no level nearby. Action?
Candlesticks = context. Without a level, they’re random noise.

Trend Identification

Higher-highs vs lower-lows. Trend is your friend.
▼

Uptrend = successive higher highs (HH) + higher lows (HL).

Downtrend = successive lower highs (LH) + lower lows (LL).

Range = equal highs and lows, no directional bias. Don't trend-trade a range.

Shortcut: price above 50 EMA on 4H = uptrend bias. Below = downtrend bias. Simple, effective.

Structure break: trend ends when an HL fails (uptrend) or an LH holds (downtrend). Wait for confirmation before flipping bias.

Simple PROSPER filter: above 4H 50 EMA = longs, below = shorts.
Uptrend breaks when...
Structure break = HL fails. That’s the only real “trend over” signal.

Breakouts & Ranges

Two market modes. Use opposite playbooks.
▼

Range mode (70% of time): price oscillates between two levels. Play mean-reversion — buy low, sell high, stops outside range.

Breakout mode (30% of time): range breaks, new trend starts. Play continuation — enter on break, SL just inside old range.

False breakout trap: 60%+ of breakouts fail on first attempt. Wait for retest, or use 15-min close confirmation.

PROSPER rule: don’t trade during the session-opening fakeout (first 30-60 min of London/NY). Wait for structure.

Risk:Reward Deep Dive

Why 1:2 R:R is the minimum worth taking.
▼

Formula: Expectancy = (WinRate × Avg Win) − (LossRate × Avg Loss).

50% WR @ 1:1 = break-even before costs. Spread + commission makes it a loser.

50% WR @ 1:2 = +0.5R per trade. Over 100 trades = +50R. On 1% risk = +50% account.

40% WR @ 1:3 = +0.8R per trade. Even better — fewer wins, bigger wins.

Golden rule: skip any setup with less than 1:2 reward potential to pre-defined TP (not a dream target).

Psychology & Emotions

The real reason 90% fail. Not strategy — discipline.
▼

Revenge trading: losing → want to “win back” → doubled size → bigger loss → repeat. Kills accounts in days.

FOMO: missing a move → chasing late → buying tops / selling bottoms. Skip the trade, take the next one.

Over-confidence after wins: 3 wins in a row → increase size → 1 big loss wipes the gain. Keep risk fixed.

Fear after losses: 3 losses → skip the next valid setup (which wins) → tilt. Trust your backtest.

Rule: 2 losses in a day → stop for the day. Walk away. Come back tomorrow. This single rule saved UkDadTrader’s career.

ADHD angle: hyperfocus + impulsivity = you will overtrade without a hard stop-count. Build the rule in, don’t “try harder”.

Chart Markup Guide

Where to draw every line. Copy this once, use it forever. Wrong markup is the #1 reason retail misreads structure.

1. Horizontal Support/Resistance

Draw through at least 3 touches. Wick touches count. Use horizontal lines (no bias).

3+ toucheswick AND bodysingle wickdrawn through noise

2. Trendlines (swing-point anchored)

Connect swing lows in uptrends (or swing highs in downtrends). Minimum 3 touches to validate.

swing points only3+ touchesforce-fit angledrawn on intraday noise

3. Fibonacci Retracement (key zones)

Anchor from swing low → swing high (uptrend) or reverse. Entries cluster at 50% / 61.8%. Below 78.6% = trend probably broken.

50% & 61.8%confluence with S/Rrandom swing

4. SL placement (the one that keeps you alive)

For longs: SL goes below the most recent swing low (or below the pattern's structure). Add a small buffer (2–5 pips FX, 0.30–0.50 XAU) to dodge stop hunts.

below swing lowsmall bufferarbitrary -20 pipstighter than structure

5. TP placement (the one that pays you)

Split TPs. TP1 at 2R or first opposing S/R — close 50%, move SL to breakeven. TP2 at the next major level. Keeps expectancy positive even when markets chop.

2R minimumscale-out1:1 targetsno TP at all

6. Order Blocks (last down-candle before the rally)

Mark a rectangle around the last bearish candle before a strong bullish breakout (or vice-versa). Price often returns to mitigate this zone — that's your high-probability re-entry.

last opposite candlestrong impulse afterany random candle

Drawing checklist — run through this every session

  • HTF first. Mark weekly + daily S/R before you touch 1h/15m.
  • Fewer lines = clearer signal. 3 strong levels beats 12 weak ones.
  • Colour code: yellow = major, cyan = minor, red = invalidation, green = TP target.
  • Never delete SL/TP once set — this breaks your pre-committed plan and is how accounts blow.
  • Mark session opens (London 08:00, NY 13:00 UTC) — liquidity sweeps cluster there.
  • Pre-mark high-impact news — NFP, CPI, FOMC. Don't trade 30m before/after.
