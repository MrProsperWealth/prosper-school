LESSON 1

The big idea

The Nasdaq 100 is a long-term uptrend machine. Inside that trend it pauses and pulls back for a day or two — profit-taking, a small shake-out — and then the bigger buyers step back in and it resumes higher.

We don't predict tops or bottoms. We confirm the trend is up, wait for a pullback, and buy only when price turns back up and proves the leg is resuming. Risk is fixed, the target is twice the risk, and we hold for a couple of days. Longs only — momentum continuation.

LESSON 2

The setup — 5 parts

One market, one setup. Read it on the 4-hour close, set a pending order, walk away.

1
Trend (daily) — daily close above the 200-day average, and the 50-day above the 200-day. The Nasdaq must be proven up. No daily uptrend = no trade. We never short this model.
2
Pullback (4H) — price dips back to the 20-period EMA, or the 4-hour RSI rolls down. A normal pause inside the trend — the discount we want to buy.
3
Trigger — a buy-STOP just above the pullback bar's high. It only fills if price turns back up and breaks higher. No resumption, no fill — that's the confirmation built in.
4
Stop — below the pullback low (about 1×ATR of room). Your disaster guard. If the pullback was actually a reversal, you're out small.
5
Target & time — take profit at 2× your risk; back-stop exit after ~5 days. Average hold ~2 days. Win ~42% — but at 2R, four wins pay for six losses and then some.
LESSON 3

The market

LESSON 5

The working-day routine

You work 8:30–4:30 with lunch 12–1. The Nasdaq 4-hour candle closes a few times across your day — glance at 4 of them, 2 minutes each:

  • Before work (~07:00) · lunch (12–1) · after work (~17:30) · evening (~21:30).
  • Setup forming? Place the buy-stop + stop + 2R pending order. Walk away.
  • Nothing there? Place nothing. ~1.4 trades a week means most glances = do nothing.
  • Log every trade — entry, exit, R, and did I follow the rules? (Y/N).

No forced trades. If there's no setup, you sit. The phone alert (when it's wired) will ping you the exact order so you never have to watch the screen.
