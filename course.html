the whole game

One box. One breakout. No marker, no trade.

XAU/USD builds an Asian range overnight. Your job is to judge whether the box is clean, wait for an H1 close outside it with daily bias and H1 EMA alignment, then plan risk mechanically. DXY stays visible as context unless a separate test proves it as a filter.

R means one unit of risk. A +2R win returns twice what you risked. A -1R loss costs exactly what you risked.

If the marker and checklist do not show the trade, the correct trade is no trade.

PO3, gap closure, DXY, UKDT-style levels, and red news can help you understand the day. They do not become funded entry rules until they beat the baseline in testing.

Marker Setup

Marker QA gate: before live or funded use, verify the Asian box, EMA labels, Action row, DXY context, news lockout, breakout-close labels, execution price labels, and stop/TP zones manually on at least 10 historical sessions.
1
SymbolOpen OANDA:XAUUSD.
2
TimeframeUse 1h. The strategy is not M5, M15, or H4.
3
IndicatorLoad PROSPER XAU H1 Asian Range Marker v2.2.
4
InputsAsian session 2300-0600 (TradingView sessions use bar OPENS — this makes the box the H1 candles closing 00:00–06:00 UK, exactly what was backtested). Watch session 0700-1900 (signal closes 08:00–19:00 UK). RR 2, max updates 8, max spike 0.65, range/ATR 0.30–3.00, max hold bars 18, keep past marks ON, DXY trade filter off, retest study labels off, shock overlay off unless deliberately enabled.
Why 2300-0600 and not 0000-0700: the validated backtest builds the box from H1 candles closing 00:00–06:00 UK. The window-shift test (lab/exp_xau_window_shift.py) shows the tested window carries OOS +0.48R vs +0.26R for the naive midnight box. The marker v2.2 also now enforces the daily SMA50/200 trend filter and the range/ATR gate that the backtest always had — if your old marker showed signals the new one skips, the new one is right. v2.2 additionally auto-marks every signal with a shaded red STOP zone and green TP zone (prices printed in the zone) and keeps past marks on the chart for review — zone length = the 18-hour max hold, so if price is still inside the zone when it ends, you're out.
state table

Read The Marker

WAIT RANGEAsian box is still building.
MESSY RANGEToo many updates, one candle dominates, or the range is outside 0.30-3.00 x ATR.
WAIT SESSIONRange ready, but watch window not open.
WAIT DAILYDaily SMA50/200 bias and H1 EMA stack are not aligned yet.
WATCH BREAKOUTClean range and bias are acceptable. Wait for H1 close.
BREAKOUT CLOSEThe tested signal has printed. Plan next-candle/open execution or journal the skip.
RETEST STUDY ONLY no fundedHidden by default. Blue dotted study lines only. No funded trade until separately tested.
RETEST STUDY ONLY has no backtest coverage for funded use. Studying the pattern on demo is fine; placing a funded trade on it is a rule breach.
COOLDOWNShock candle recently fired. Duration is marker-defined. If unsure, skip the session.
ROLLOVERLate-session warning. Spreads widen. Your broker's rollover time may differ; check the exact server-time window before live use.
LOCKED NEWSManual red-news lockout is on. Cross-check Forex Factory or another live calendar; this is not automatic.
v2.2 visuals: the Asian box is a clear teal outline. EMA 20, EMA 50, and EMA 200 are labelled at the right edge. On a signal, the execution label shows EXEC @ price. The shaded red STOP zone shows the entry-to-stop risk (-1R), and the shaded green TP zone shows entry-to-target (+2R). Prices are printed inside the zones.
18h time exit: the STOP and TP zones run 18 H1 bars to the right of entry. If price is still inside the zones when they end, the trade is closed. The right edge of the zones is the time exit.
Action row rule: the Action row is a plain-English summary of the checklist output, not a replacement for the checklist. BUY next H1 open or SELL next H1 open means the marker has summarised a valid pending execution. WAIT - watch BUY/SELL break means no signal yet. TRADE TAKEN - no re-entry means the day's trade is already used. DON'T TRADE means stand down.
News routine: check the calendar before London open and again before any planned entry. FOMC/NFP-class red events create a 4h lockout. Do not enter after the event to catch a move that already happened.

Before work: screenshot the box and state table. If messy, log no trade.

Lunch 12:15-13:00: check top-of-hour close and alert state. No trade during calls.

State changed? If it changes to MESSY, LOCKED NEWS, or COOLDOWN between checks, treat the session as no-trade.

After work: journal the decision. Good process first, payouts later.

candidate lane

H4 London Breakout — Study/Demo Only

Promoted from the lab on 2026-07-04 (lab/exp_breakout_h4.py, pre-registered gates). It is not the funded model. It runs beside the H1 Asian model as a demo-forward candidate because it fixes the cadence problem: about 8 signals a month across XAU + NAS100 versus 1.9 on H1 alone.

Tested rules (frozen for study):

1. Mark the London morning range 08:00–12:00 UK (same box discipline as the Asian range).
2. Wait for an H4 candle CLOSE outside the range — closes land 13:00 / 17:00 / 21:00 UK. A wick is not a signal, same as H1.
3. Daily trend + EMA stack must agree with the break direction (same filter family as the funded model).
4. Stop: 1.0 × H4 ATR from entry. Target: 1.5R. Max hold 2 days. One new trade per market per day. 4h news block.
5. Skip shock candles (H4 range > 3 × ATR) and micro ranges (< 0.5 × ATR).

Backtest (8.5y, both markets, net of costs): 816 trades · OOS +0.33R · OOS PF 1.71 · 2026 holdout n=48 at +0.15R · FTMO sim ~99% pass, 0% breach at 0.25% risk.

Honest flags: XAU's own 2026 leg is negative (−0.16R, the holdout is carried by NAS100), and out-of-sample beat in-sample, which usually means recent regimes flatter it. Treat every live signal as evidence collection, not income.
Promotion to funded core requires: 3 months of demo-forward signals journalled, realized expectancy ≥ 50% of backtest, and the XAU leg proving itself in the current year. Until then it is a rule breach to fund this lane.

learning science

How This Course Makes It Stick

  • Dual coding: every rule is paired with a visual chart cue.
  • Worked examples: see the full trade plan before doing drills alone.
  • Retrieval practice: cards force recall before rereading.
  • Interleaving: drills mix clean range, wick traps, news blocks, work interruptions, and 2R decisions.
  • Spacing: card ratings now schedule the next review for today, tomorrow, 3 days, or 7 days.
  • Immediate feedback: practice reps show which rule passed or blocked the setup.
