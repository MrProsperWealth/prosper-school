LESSON 1

The big idea

Markets are driven by fear and greed. In a healthy uptrend, an occasional sharp 1–2 day sell-off is usually fear, not a real change of trend — forced sellers, margin calls, panic. Big index buyers step in and price snaps back to its average within days.

We're not predicting. We wait for the trend to be proven, wait for the rare flush, buy the fear with defined risk, and leave the moment it reverts. High win-rate, small targets, tight discipline.

LESSON 2

The 4 gates

All four must be green. Read them on the daily close, place a pending order for tomorrow, walk away.

1
Uptrend — daily close above the 200-day average, and the 50-day above the 200-day. The market must be proven up. No uptrend = no trade, ever.
2
The dip — RSI(2) below 5. A 2-period RSI under 5 is a rare, violent oversold flush — the fear we buy. RSI(14) is too slow; RSI(2) catches the 1–2 day panic.
3
Entry — buy-limit at the close minus 0.10×ATR; stop 1.0×ATR below. Set it the evening before. Valid one day — if it doesn't fill, cancel. The stop is your disaster guard; it's rarely hit.
4
Exit — when price reverts: close back above the 5-day average OR RSI(2) above 70. Backstop: 5-day time-stop.You don't chase. The bounce reverts to the mean — take it and reset.
LESSON 3

The markets

Index CFDs only — the dip-snap-back is an equity-index property. But not every index has it. We tested each one across 8.5 years with a hidden 2026 hold-out. Only three were positive in every single period — trade those:

  • US2000 (Russell 2000) — the statistical best: 71% win, +0.44R, profit factor 2.49.
  • SPX500 (S&P 500) — your steady core: 58% win, +0.38R, tightest spread, closes right in your evening window.
  • JP225 (Nikkei) — 67% win, +0.38R (closes in your morning).

Don't be tempted by NAS100 or DAX — popular, but they failed the hold-out test. Discipline means trading what the data supports, not what's famous. US2000 + SPX500 are your two core (both US-session, both robust).

Why not forex or gold? They don't reliably bounce from dips — they trend or chop. Tested across 23 markets and 3,680 backtests: no edge. Indices are where it lives.

LESSON 4

Risk & FTMO

FTMO 2-Step Swing £10k (verified 2026): make +10% (Phase 1) then +5% (Phase 2), never lose −5% in a day or −10% total. Minimum 4 trading days each phase (easily met). 80% profit split, paid every 14 days once funded.

  • 0.5% risk per trade (safe) up to 1% (faster, more variance).
  • Max 1% open at once — so ≤2 positions at 0.5%.
  • Personal −1.5% daily stop — walk away for the day, even though FTMO allows −5%.

Swing account perk: you're exempt from news-trading restrictions and may hold over weekends. So skipping event days is your optional discipline, not an FTMO rule.

UK time: set your TradingView chart to London. The US indices' daily candle closes ~21:00 UK — right inside your ~21:30 evening check, so you act on a fresh close.

The honest truth: this edge is very safe (drawdowns a fraction of the limit, ~95–100% pass probability) but not fast at its natural ~1–2 trades/month. Treat passing as the by-product of clean execution — not a sprint.
